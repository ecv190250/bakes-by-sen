As someone who is purely passionate about food, there is nothing I love more than building a dessert entirely from scratch. You don't need a massive, industrial kitchen to make beautiful pastries—you just need a little patience and a love for the process.

I designed these tarts to have a specific split look: one half bright, fresh blueberry compote, and the other half rich custard topped with toasted almonds. This recipe breaks down exactly how I make them in my own kitchen, step-by-step, with no shortcuts.

### Part 1: My Homemade Shortcrust (The Foundation)

The secret to a crisp, flaky crust is keeping your ingredients cold. If the butter melts before it hits the oven, the crust will shrink and become tough.

**Ingredients:**

- **¾ cup (95g)** All-Purpose Flour
- **¼ cup (56g)** Unsalted Butter, _very cold and cut into tiny cubes_
- **2 tbsp** Powdered Sugar
- **1** Large Egg Yolk
- **1-2 tsp** Ice Water

**The Step-by-Step Process:**

1. **The "Rub In":** In a bowl, whisk the flour and powdered sugar. Add the cold butter cubes. Using just your fingertips, quickly rub the butter into the flour until the mixture looks like coarse sand with a few pea-sized butter pieces left.
2. **The Bind:** Add the egg yolk and 1 teaspoon of ice water. Gently mix with a fork until the dough just starts to clump together. If it is too dry, add the second teaspoon of water.
3. **Chill (Crucial Step!):** Form the dough into a flat disc, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap, and put it in the fridge for at least 30 minutes.
4. **Roll and Shape:** Lightly flour your counter. Roll the dough out until it is about ⅛-inch thick. Cut out 3 circles slightly larger than your mini-tart tins.
5. **Press and Dock:** Gently lay the dough into the tins. Press it into the corners without stretching it. Take a fork and gently poke a few holes in the bottom of the dough to let steam escape. Place the tins in the freezer for 15 minutes.
6. **Blind Baking:** Preheat the oven to **375°F (190°C)**. To stop the crusts from puffing up, crumple a small piece of parchment paper, place it inside each tart shell, and fill the paper with dry beans, rice, or ceramic pie weights.
7. **The Bake:** Bake for 12 minutes with the weights in. Then, carefully remove the parchment and weights, and bake for another 3 to 5 minutes until the bottom is a dry, pale golden brown. Let them cool completely.

### Part 2: The Vanilla Custard (The Filling)

This process uses a technique called "tempering" to ensure your egg yolks turn into a smooth cream rather than scrambled eggs.

**Ingredients:**

- **1 cup** Whole Milk
- **2** Large Egg Yolks
- **3 tbsp** Granulated Sugar
- **1 ½ tbsp** Cornstarch
- **1 tsp** Vanilla Extract
- **1 tbsp** Butter (optional, for a glossy finish)

**The Step-by-Step Process:**

1. **Heat the Milk:** Pour the milk into a small saucepan and heat it gently over medium heat until it is steaming and small bubbles form around the edges. Do not let it boil.
2. **Whisk the Base:** While the milk heats, vigorously whisk the egg yolks, sugar, and cornstarch in a bowl until the mixture is pale and completely smooth.
3. **Temper the Eggs:** Slowly pour about half of the hot milk into the egg mixture in a very thin stream, whisking constantly.
4. **Thicken:** Pour the entire egg and milk mixture back into the saucepan. Turn the heat to medium-low. Whisk continuously. The mixture will suddenly thicken into a pudding-like consistency. Let it bubble for 30 seconds to cook out the raw starch flavor, then remove from heat.
5. **Finish and Chill:** Whisk in the vanilla extract and the butter. Pour the hot custard into a clean bowl. Press a piece of plastic wrap _directly_ onto the surface of the custard to prevent a rubbery skin from forming. Chill in the fridge until cold and firm.

### Part 3: The Blueberry Compote (The Brightness)

**Ingredients:**

- **½ cup** Fresh Blueberries
- **1 tbsp** Granulated Sugar
- **1 tsp** Water or Lemon Juice

**The Step-by-Step Process:**

1. Place the berries, sugar, and liquid in a small pot over medium heat.
2. Stir frequently as the berries begin to burst and release their juices.
3. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes until it looks thick and jammy. Transfer to a small bowl and let it cool completely.

### Part 4: Assembly & The Flavor Harmony

**Ingredients:**

- **2 tbsp** Almond Flakes

**The Step-by-Step Process:**

1. **Toast the Almonds:** Place a dry frying pan over medium heat. Add the almond flakes and toss them for 1 to 2 minutes until they are fragrant and lightly browned. Remove from the pan immediately so they don't burn.
2. **Whisk the Custard:** Take your chilled custard out of the fridge and whisk it vigorously for a few seconds to make it perfectly smooth and creamy again.
3. **Fill the Shells:** Spoon or pipe the custard generously into your cooled tart shells.
4. **The Split Design:** Spoon the cooled blueberry compote over exactly one-half of the tart's surface.
5. **The Crunch:** Sprinkle the toasted almond flakes over the exposed custard half.
