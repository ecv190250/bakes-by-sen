Some days, you don't need fancy flavors or complicated decorations. You just need a great, classic vanilla cake. This is my "Everyday Cake"—it’s light, fluffy, and comes together so quickly that it’s become my absolute favorite for busy afternoons.

Whether you enjoy it warm with a drizzle of honey, topped with fresh berries, or just plain with your morning coffee, this recipe never misses.

### The "Go-To" Vanilla Cake

**Ingredients:**

- **125g** All-Purpose Flour
- **60ml** Vegetable Oil (or melted butter)
- **100g** White Granulated Sugar
- **1** Large Egg
- **1 tsp** Baking Powder
- **1 pinch** Salt
- **120ml** Milk
- **½ tsp** Vanilla Extract

### Instructions:

1. **Prep:** Preheat your oven to **350°F (175°C)** and grease your cake tin.
2. **Whisk the Wet:** In a bowl, combine the egg, sugar, oil (or melted butter), milk, and vanilla extract. Whisk until the sugar is fully dissolved and the mixture is smooth.
3. **Add the Dry:** Gently stir in the flour, baking powder, and the pinch of salt. Mix until the batter is just combined—avoid over-mixing to keep the cake nice and light!
4. **Bake:** Pour into your tin and bake for approximately 25–30 minutes, or until a tester comes out clean.

### Baker's Note

If you want to dress this up, this base is incredibly forgiving. You can fold in some fresh blueberries, sprinkle some lemon zest into the batter, or even add a simple sugar glaze once it cools.

**This is the cake that reminds me why I started baking: simple ingredients, minimal effort, and a delicious result every time.**

### Pro-Tip for Texture

Since this recipe uses both oil/butter and milk, it stays moist for days. If you're planning to store it, keep it in an airtight container at room temperature. It’s actually often _better_ on the second day after the flavors have had time to settle!
