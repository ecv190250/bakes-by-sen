I make a lot of high-end desserts, but this basic all-purpose flour cake is something I keep coming back to. It’s consistent, it doesn't break the bank, and it delivers every single time

**Basic All-Purpose Flour Cake: A Practical Guide**

This recipe is designed for reliability and consistency in a professional or home kitchen setting. It provides a foundational sponge that is easy to prepare and versatile for different applications.

**Ingredients**

 **125 g all-purpose flour**: The structural base of the cake.

 **100 g sugar**: Provides sweetness and aids in moisture retention.

 **120 ml milk**: Adds moisture and contributes to a tender crumb.

 **60 ml oil or melted butter**: The primary fat source.

 **1 egg**: Acts as the binding agent for structure.

 **1 tsp baking powder**: The leavening agent responsible for the rise.

 **1/2 tsp vanilla (optional)**: Enhances the overall flavor profile.

 **Pinch of salt**: Balances the sweetness.

Instructions

1. **Preheat**: Set your oven to 180°C to ensure an even bake from the start.
2. **Whisk**: Combine the egg and sugar in a mixing bowl until the mixture is uniform.
3. **Liquids**: Incorporate the milk, oil (or melted butter), and vanilla, ensuring they are fully integrated.
4. **Dry**: Add the flour, baking powder, and salt.
5. **Mix**: Mix the batter gently until the dry ingredients are just combined. Avoid overmixing, as this can develop excessive gluten, leading to a tougher cake texture.
6. **Pan**: Pour the mixture into a greased pan to prevent sticking.
7. **Bake**: Bake for 25–30 minutes, or until the cake is golden and fully set.

Professional Insights for Better Results

Understanding your ingredients allows you to troubleshoot and customize your bakes:

 **Softness**: For a more delicate, tender crumb, replace 20 g of the all-purpose flour with 20 g of cornstarch. This reduces the total protein content, resulting in a less chewy texture.

 **Moisture vs. Flavor**: Selecting your fat is a trade-off. Use oil if your primary goal is maximizing moisture, or use melted butter if you prefer a richer, more traditional dairy flavor.

 **Presentation**: Regardless of the simplicity of the cake, professional packaging elevates the final product. As seen in **image above**, neat piping and secure boxing ensure the cake is presented cleanly to the customer.
